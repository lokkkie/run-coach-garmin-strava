# Methodology: Pfitzinger (Mileage-Heavy Marathon)

## The core idea

Pete Pfitzinger's marathon plans (from *Advanced Marathoning*) are built on **high mileage, lactate-threshold work, and the medium-long run**. The thesis: marathon performance is dominated by aerobic capacity and the ability to sustain effort for 3+ hours, both of which respond to mileage more than to interval work.

These plans are demanding. They assume the runner can train 5–6×/week and recover well. Done right, they produce big marathon improvements; done with insufficient base, they produce injuries.

## When to use Pfitzinger

- **Marathon goals only** — Pfitz isn't designed for shorter races (use Daniels)
- **Peak weekly mileage will exceed 60 km** — below 60 km/wk, the volume signature of Pfitzinger isn't there
- **Kevin can train 5–6×/week** — 4×/week can't fit the medium-long run + long run + threshold + recovery
- **Established base** — at least 6 months consistent running with no recent injuries
- **Marathon goal is sub-3:30 or better, OR** the runner just wants a strong finish from a well-developed base

## When NOT to use Pfitzinger

- 5K/10K/half goals → Daniels
- Peak weekly mileage staying below 50 km → 80/20 or Daniels
- < 4 days/week training capacity → Daniels with shorter long runs
- Recent injury history that responds poorly to high volume → 80/20 with conservative progression

## Plan templates

Pfitz's book defines plans by **weeks × peak weekly mileage in miles**. Convert to km:

| Plan | Weeks | Peak mileage (mi/wk) | Peak mileage (km/wk) |
|------|-------|---------------------|---------------------|
| 12/55 | 12 | 55 | ~88 |
| 18/55 | 18 | 55 | ~88 |
| 18/70 | 18 | 70 | ~113 |
| 18/85 | 18 | 85 | ~137 |
| 18/100+ | 18 | 100+ | ~160+ |

For Kevin's likely range (first or second marathon, sub-4:00 or sub-3:30 goal), 18/55 is the realistic starting Pfitz plan. 18/70 if he has a strong base and is targeting sub-3:30.

## Key sessions

### The medium-long run (this is what makes Pfitzinger Pfitzinger)

- Distance: 18–24 km
- Pace: easy throughout (E pace from Daniels VDOT, or 80–90% of long-run pace)
- Day: midweek (typically Wednesday)
- Purpose: cumulative aerobic stimulus without the recovery cost of a long run

The medium-long is the session most newer Pfitz runners skip or shorten. Don't. It's not optional — it's the structural backbone.

### The long run

- Distance: progressive to 32–35 km
- Pace: easy with optional M-pace blocks in peak phase (e.g., last 12 km @ M pace on a 26 km long run)
- Day: Sunday (always)

Pfitz long runs cap at 32–35 km regardless of marathon goal. Running 38 km in training adds injury risk without commensurate fitness gain.

### Lactate threshold (LT) sessions

- Format: 20–60 min continuous at LT pace (≈ T pace from Daniels)
- 4–8 km at threshold within a longer run is the standard format
- Frequency: 1×/week throughout most of the plan

### General aerobic runs

- Distance: 8–14 km
- Pace: easy (E)
- Frequency: 2–3×/week

These are the "filler" runs that bring weekly volume to spec.

### VO2max intervals

- Format: 5 × 1000m @ 5K pace, 3 min recovery (typical)
- Frequency: weekly during weeks 8–14 of an 18-week plan
- Total volume: 4–6 km of intervals per session

### Recovery runs

- Distance: 5–8 km
- Pace: very easy (slower than E)
- Day: morning after a hard session

Pfitz uses recovery runs liberally. They keep weekly volume up without compounding fatigue.

## Sample week (18/55 plan, week 10 of 18, ~78 km)

| Day | Session | Distance |
|-----|---------|----------|
| Mon | Rest or recovery 5 km | 0–5 km |
| Tue | LT: 14 km w/ 6 km @ LT | 14 km |
| Wed | Medium-long: 18 km | 18 km |
| Thu | Recovery 8 km | 8 km |
| Fri | General aerobic 11 km + 6 strides | 11 km |
| Sat | Recovery 6 km | 6 km |
| Sun | Long: 26 km w/ last 10 km @ M | 26 km |

Weekly total: ~83 km (slightly above 55 mi peak — peak weeks routinely overshoot).

## Phase structure (18-week plan)

| Phase | Weeks | Focus |
|-------|-------|-------|
| Endurance | 1–6 | Build aerobic base, peak medium-long → 22 km |
| Lactate threshold + endurance | 7–12 | Add LT volume, peak long → 30 km |
| Race preparation | 13–15 | M-pace work, tune-up race optional |
| Taper | 16–18 | Volume cut, sharpening |

## Common mistakes

- **Skipping the medium-long.** As above. Plan won't work without it.
- **Running general aerobic too fast.** Pfitz general aerobic is closer to E than to "moderate." If it's the same pace as the long run, that's right.
- **Adding intervals from elsewhere.** Pfitz plans are designed as a closed system. Don't bolt on track sessions from a Daniels plan because they "look easy."
- **Overshooting peak mileage.** The peak number is a peak, not a target to exceed. If the plan says 88 km that week, run 88 km — not 100.
